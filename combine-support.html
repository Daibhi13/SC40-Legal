Combine Analysis — Support

Combine Test Performance Tracker for iOS.

Getting Started

Getting Started

Tap 'New Session' to log a combine testing or training session. Enter the session details, surface, location, RPE and notes, then add reps for each test you complete. You can also log drills and strides completed during the session.

Combine Tests

Log results for the six combine tests: 40 Yard Dash, 10 Yard Split, Vertical Jump, Broad Jump, Agility Drill and Bench Press. For sprint-based tests you can add splits and run type. Each test records its value, rest time and any notes.

Session Types

Tag each session as Testing or Training using the category selector. Testing sessions are used for personal best and fitness level tracking. Training sessions are used for workout logging and trend analysis.

Drills & Strides

Log drills (High Knees, Butt Kicks, etc.) and stride distances completed during your session. Tap 'Add Drill' or 'Add Stride' and set the number of reps for each.

Analysis & Scoring

Fitness Levels

Combine Analysis classifies each result into a fitness level (Elite, Advanced, Intermediate, Beginner) based on age-graded benchmarks for the test. Your overall profile is shown on the Home dashboard.

Personal Bests

Personal bests are automatically detected when you log a better result than your current PB for a given test. PBs are tracked across all six combine tests. You can also manually enter or edit your PBs in Edit Profile (More tab).

Edit Profile

Update your name, age, weight, height, sport, position, and personal bests anytime from the More tab → Edit Profile. All fields entered during onboarding are editable.

Trends & Analytics (Pro)

Combine Analysis Pro unlocks advanced trend analysis, test-by-test breakdowns, personal best tracking and comparison, coach feedback, training log filters, and performance report sharing. A 14-day free trial may be offered, then a subscription.

Timing Accuracy

Manual timing is less accurate than electronic systems. Hand timing typically adds 0.1-0.3s compared to fully automated timing. Use consistent timing methods for meaningful trend analysis.

Data & Privacy

Data & Privacy

Your workout data is stored locally on your device. When you log a session with location enabled, your approximate location coordinates are sent to a weather service to fetch temperature, humidity, and wind conditions for that session. No personal data or test results are sent to any server. You can use the app without location services — just enter weather conditions manually.
